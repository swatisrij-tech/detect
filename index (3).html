What is Sepsis?

Sepsis is a life-threatening medical emergency that occurs when the body's response to an infection spirals out of control, damaging its own tissues and organs. It can develop rapidly and may lead to septic shock, multi-organ failure, and death if not treated immediately. Early recognition and treatment are critical — every hour of delay increases mortality risk significantly.
